### Describe your project

The Free Motion Capture Project (FreeMoCap) aims to provide research-grade markerless motion capture software to everyone for free.

FreeMoCap is a user-friendly framework that connects an array of ‘bleeding edge’ open-source tools from the computer vision and machine learning communities to accurately record full-body 3D movement of humans, animals, robots, and other objects. 

We will make the newly emerging mind-boggling, future-shaping technologies that drive FreeMoCap’s core functionality accessible to communities of people who stand to benefit from them. We follow a “Universal Design” development philosophy, with the goal of creating a system that serves the needs of a professional research scientist while remaining intuitive to a 13-year-old with no technical training and no outside assistance.

The FreeMoCap System uses a generic camera interface to remain compatible with everything from research-quality cameras to GoPro’s to bottom-shelf usb webcams. A minimal setup can be achieved with a standard PC or laptop and two $20 usb webcams.

A high-quality, minimal-cost motion capture system would be a transformative tool for a wide range of communities \- including 3d animators, game designers, athletes, coaches, performers, scientists, engineers, clinicians, and doctors. We hope to create a system that brings new technological capacity to these groups while also building bridges between them.

Everyone has a reason to record human movement. We want to help them do it.

### Describe your next steps and plan to develop your project

The FreeMoCap system currently exists as a stable prototype software that reliably produces functional output in the hands of expert users **\[see video\].** 

We are currently engaged in the activities listed below to develop this project. This proposal seeks funding to support Activity \#1. The other activities are presently supported by other means. 

1) Contract an experienced software developer to scale the prototype into an enterprise-ready software amenable to long-term growth and wide-spread distribution. **\[See Funds Requested\]**  
     
2) Work with professional 3d animators to develop automated rigging and mesh retargeting methods for FreeMoCap output to facilitate use by the animation community and compatibility with tools such as Unreal Engine, Blender, and Unity.  
     
3) Validate FreeMoCap system using the traditional motion capture system in Jonathan Samir **Matthis’** lab at Northeastern University (\~1300 sq ft/120sqm, 32 Qualisys cameras, 5 AMTI force plates, and a Bertec instrumented treadmill). \[Funded by NIH/NEI R00-EY028229 awarded to **Matthis**\]  
     
4) Develop connections and plan collaborations with other aligned projects (including other MegaGrant recipients such as the Blender Foundation and Kara Technologies)  
     
5) Attain federal IRS 501c3 tax exempt status for the FreeMoCap Foundation, a non-profit corporation formed to support the FreeMoCap Project.
