## Expected value
*If the proposal is successfully funded, what does success look like? We're seeking to understand: what type of capabilities the proposal is unlocking for the scientific community; how upstream and downstream software will be improved by the proposal; how the proposed work supports or implements novel functionality for AI enablement and large-scale data analysis. (1,500 characters maximum)*

Our goal is to build the best motion capture software the world has ever seen at any price.

Success means any researcher, clinician, student, or artist with a handful of cameras can produce research-grade full-body kinematics measurements once locked behind six-figure marker-based labs or expensive subscriptions. A clinician assessing gait, a scientist studying perceptual-motor control, or a coach quantifying athletic movement could capture validated 3D data using hardware they already own, with no per-use fees or vendor lock-in. 

Upstream, FreeMoCap gives computer-vision and pose-estimation researchers a calibrated multi-camera platform to deploy and validate new tracking models in real capture volumes. Downstream, it produces standardized 3D outputs with quality metadata ready for biomechanics, clinical, and statistical pipelines.

For AI enablement and large-scale analysis, we will make FreeMoCap fully programmable and batch-capable, so researchers can process large recording sets, build movement datasets across subjects and conditions, and integrate capture into automated workflows — letting advances in AI-based tracking reach movement researchers everywhere through shared, validated, open infrastructure.

Practically speaking, the proposal will support our transition from the current `v1.*` architecture to the far more advanced architecure of FreeMoCap `v2.*` that will allow us to grow into a global scale enterprise software with widespread use and developer support.
