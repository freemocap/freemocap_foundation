## Short summary
*Briefly describe the purpose of the proposal and the software project(s) it involves. 3,000 characters maximum* 

The FreeMoCap Project, or FreeMoCap, is a free, open-source markerless motion-capture platform that lets researchers turn ordinary cameras into a 3D measurement system for movement, enabling the capture of human or animal behavior without specialized motion-capture hardware or proprietary software. Using AI-based image tracking and synchronized camera footage, FreeMoCap generates research-grade 3D movement data for visualization, measurement, and analysis. With standard cameras and open software, FreeMoCap makes quantitative motion analysis accessible to labs, clinics, classrooms and field sites without access to motion capture systems that traditionally cost $50,000–$200,000 or more. FreeMoCap is the core software project of the FreeMoCap Foundation, a nonprofit organization supporting open, accessible tools for scientific, educational, and technological use.

Advances in computer vision and AI have made it possible to track movement from video, but most existing markerless motion-capture tools remain limited to fixed human models or narrow analysis workflows. This limits their usefulness for researchers studying nonstandard human populations, animal behavior, or task-specific movements that require custom landmarks or tracking models. This proposal will support continued development of FreeMoCap as both an accessible end-user tool and a more general-purpose framework for AI-enabled 3D motion capture across the life sciences. 

Our preliminary work has already shown the benefits of an extensible, adaptable motion capture alternative. When analyzing gait and posture, integration of multiple human pose estimation models into our pipeline and subsequent validation against marker-based motion capture showed that the choice of AI model can meaningfully affect downstream measurement accuracy. We have also extended FreeMoCap to support custom-trained DeepLabCut models, with one such encompassing 3D tracking of gaze, posture, and motor behavior in freely moving juvenile ferrets, with planned integration alongside neural recordings and 1P microscopy to study how brain activity and behavior co-develop. This proposal aims to convert these extensions into robust, documented, reusable pathways for researchers who need custom tracking models, skeletons, keypoints, or validation workflows.

Funding will move these capabilities from demonstrated research extensions into stable, community-usable software: pathways for adding custom AI tracking models, defining new skeletons and keypoint sets, processing recordings at scale, exporting standardized 3D data with quality metrics, and benchmarking workflows so users can evaluate whether a pipeline is appropriate for their organism, task, or scientific question. Together, these tools will support large-scale datasets, reproducible analyses, and downstream AI workflows while making FreeMoCap a general-purpose open platform for 3D motion capture across the life sciences.

## Expected value

Success means a life-sciences researcher can use FreeMoCap to capture 3D movement data for organisms and contexts that current markerless tools do not support. A neuroscientist studying freely moving animals, a clinician assessing gait with a prosthetic limb, or an ecologist recording locomotion in a field setting could use an AI tracking model suited to their subject within FreeMoCap’s calibrated reconstruction pipeline, validate its accuracy, and produce standardized 3D data. Today, this often requires building custom infrastructure from scratch - this proposal makes it an open, supported workflow.

Upstream, the project gives AI model developers a direct path into calibrated 3D research workflows. Downstream, it produces structured outputs with quality metadata for biomechanics analyses, behavioral pipelines, statistical workflows, and machine-learning systems. Benchmarking tools will help researchers evaluate whether a model and camera configuration are appropriate for their organism, task, or scientific question before committing to a full study.

The work supports AI enablement and large-scale data analysis by making FreeMoCap programmable and batch-capable, so researchers can process large recording sets, build movement datasets across subjects and conditions, and integrate FreeMoCap into scripted or automated workflows. The result is that advances in AI-based tracking, wherever they originate, can reach movement researchers across the life sciences through shared, validated, open infrastructure.

Researchers who need quantitative 3D movement data typically rely on proprietary marker-based systems such as Vicon, Qualisys, Motion Analysis, and OptiTrack, or commercial markerless systems such as Theia3D. These tools are mature and widely used, but they are costly, closed-source, and difficult for researchers to modify, audit, or extend. FreeMoCap has demonstrated substantial adoption by the community, with over 12,000 users across approximately 140 countries (based on optional telemetry data since September 2022), over 8,000 Github stars and near 800 Github forks, and an active forum community of over 3,000 members on Discord. Independent publications span reaching kinematics, gait mechanics, jump validation, clinical gait assessment, and computational linguistics. 

This proposal builds on FreeMoCap’s existing adoption by strengthening the parts of the software that make open-source scientific use possible: clear interfaces, reproducible benchmarks, quality metrics, and contribution pathways. As AI pose-estimation models evolve, these tools will help researchers evaluate which models are appropriate for their specific movement tasks and make validated improvements available to the broader community.
